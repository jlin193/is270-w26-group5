    Overview

    This specialization dossier serves as a collection of learnings from a conversation with Yvonne Lee, the Director of Integrated Systems at The Huntington.

    When working in digital asset management (DAM), young professionals must understand that most problems aren’t “technology problems,” they’re “people problems.” Budding library and information studies (LIS) professionals don’t need to have an extensive list of programming and technology skills. However, as LIS practitioners, we must be able to support people through technology, through digital means, and through data, because that’s often how answers are quantified and communicated.

    In today’s world, where AI is explosively popular and GLAM (galleries, libraries, archives, and museums) professionals fear for the end of their careers, Yvonne sheds some light on why LIS professionals still and will continue to exist. DAMs isn’t just about organizing and filing in the right place, on the right device—rather, DAMs help bridge the gap between data and people. In large institutions like The Huntington, whose collections are vast and whose visitors overlap with each other's departments, it’s all the more important for entities to be communicative and collaborative for everyone’s benefit. Numerous resources exist to support those who wish to be involved and informed on all things technology and DAMs related, and research will only improve as more technological products are released. Therefore, as future information professionals, technology is not something we should be afraid of.

    Data and the arts do not have to be separate entities, and both can actually supplement each other’s existence. Not only does Yvonne work with collections and their data, but her role would not be possible without her appreciation for cultural preservation, art history, and informatics. Yvonne was transparent about how much her career has grown and changed. While the scarcity of GLAM jobs is a real fear amongst emerging professionals, steadfast persistence and a pursuit for growth can get you to a role that aligns with your interests.
