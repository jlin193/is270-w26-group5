Professional Context:

Job-market outlook

Digital Asset Management (DAMs) work is industry-agnostic, which can be both a blessing and a curse.

The DAMs field is growing; skills are highly transferable across industries. More and more GLAM institutions in particular are digitizing their collections, increasing the need for professionals who understand the importance of art and culture preservation while having the technical know-how to make them accessible.

However, many corporate and private settings often don’t require an MLIS degree, which makes the job market highly competitive. Outside of archival and research work, LIS professionals will compete with professionals who have background(s) in finance, data analytics, business operations, and/or project management.


Institutional and political constraints

“As Robert Weisberg [from Museum Human] says, ‘Any project or initiative will always need TMPR, time, money, people, and resources.’ Nonprofits in the US are largely funded by a mix of earned and contributed revenue which are absolutely part of the larger economic landscape. The freezing of IMLS funding last year and its latest iteration was/is disruptive and greatly affected the work this sector does.”
