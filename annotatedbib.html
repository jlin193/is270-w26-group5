Annotated Bibliography:

  1. Cloud, Experience. “Digital Asset Management (DAM) — What It Is, How It Works, and Best Solutions.” Adobe.com, 2023, https://business.adobe.com/blog/basics/digital-asset-management
    2. This corporate blog source provided the dossier with a foundational understanding of the interviewee’s field of expertise: digital asset management (DAMs). The material emphasizes values and goals shared by The Huntington: “to enhance the efficiency, security, and accessibility of digital assets.” While it promotes its own AI resources such as Adobe Firefly, it additionally provides basic definitions and examples for terms such as “digital/creative asset” and jobs that use DAMs.
  1. Joyce, Déirdre, et al. “Toward a Conceptual Framework for Technical Debt in Archives.” The American Archivist, vol. 85, no. 1, 2022, pp. 104–25. JSTOR, https://www.jstor.org/stable/48829099. Accessed 12 Mar. 2026.
    2. Published in The American Archivist in 2022, Joyce, Déirdre, et al’s journal article on “technical debt” in archives expanded on the digital repercussions of subjective decision making to keep up with modern standards, while recognizing projects will inevitably accrue debt, with the intention to implement digital payback strategies. This article was relevant to Yvonne Lee’s description of The Huntington’s newly launched online collections discovery portal, synthesizing their library art and botanical collections in one unified catalog. Lee discussed the imperfections of the search data process, favoring the quantity of botanical library prose over visual botanical references.
  1. Lee, Yvonne. Personal Interview. By Joy Pagdilao, 27 Feb. 2026.
    2. Yvonne Lee, Director of Integrated Systems at The Huntington, was interviewed by Joy Pagdilao over Zoom for IS 270: Systems and Infrastructure. She disclosed her journey through varying careers, starting with art galleries, to a graffiti archiving project (The Placa Project, by Steve Grody), UCLA’s MLIS program, various consultant jobs, leading to well known LIS industry institutions: LACMA and The Huntington. Lee’s anecdotal accounts of her career were valuable because she provided us with numerous resources in the job market, local conferences, digital forums, and educational recommendations.
  1. Michalak, Russell, and Devon Ellixson. “Addressing Language and Ableism in Information Technology: A Call to Action for Academic Librarians.” Journal of Library Administration, vol. 65, no. 1, Jan. 2025, pp. 100–31. EBSCOhost, https://doi.org/10.1080/01930826.2024.2432225.
    2. Michalak and Ellixson’s article advocates for accessible information and inclusion of the disabled community in libraries. The material contains a section “Ableism in digital asset management systems (DAMS),” highlighting issues such as inaccessible metadata, non-accessible interfaces, and limited format options. Strategies to decrease ableism in this subfield include avoiding ableist language, using tags and keywords, mobile and tablet compatibility, and regular accessibility training. This article is relevant to our research because of the inequitable issues we’ve encountered such as misogyny, lack of diversity, and ableist interphase.
  1. van Strien, Daniel, et al. “An Introduction to AI for GLAM.” Proceedings of the Second Teaching Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence Workshop, edited by Katherine M. Kinnaird et al., vol. 170, Proceedings of Machine Learning Research, 2022, pp. 20–24. https://proceedings.mlr.press/v170/strien22a/strien22a.pdf
    2. This article served us as a guide to understanding the broad scope of “machine learning based research and projects within the GLAM sector.” van Strien et al. discusses library carpentry (digital software data skills development), a class Yvonne Lee took at Caltech and recommended for the subfield. The article advocates for introductory machine learning (ML) training for the LIS field from pooled resources, not just from one singular institution.
